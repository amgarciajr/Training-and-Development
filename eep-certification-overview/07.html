Anti-Gaming & Calibration Integrity

Evidence vs. Self-Report
Evidence is observable, verifiable, and independently confirmable. A pre-send review log with specific examples is evidence. "I always apply the three tests" is self-report — and self-report does not qualify.
Calibration Drift
Drift occurs when all practitioners in a team accumulate "At Standard" or "Above Standard" ratings without observable change in delivery quality. Uniform upward movement without behavioral evidence is the signal.
Corrective Protocol
When drift is detected: acknowledge the pattern explicitly, return to rubric language, and require specific behavioral examples for each rating. Do not adjust ratings without documented behavioral evidence.
The Evidence Standard
The test is simple: if it doesn't exist independently of the claim, it's self-report. Ratings must be anchored to artifacts, logs, or observed behaviors — not to the practitioner's own account of their performance.
EEP Certification Path § 3.3 — Anti-Gaming Guidance: Any rubric with advancement implications is vulnerable to gaming. Calibration integrity is a shared responsibility of Leads and Trainers.
