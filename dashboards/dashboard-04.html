CSAM Partnership Principles — EEP Standard
1
Make the CSAM look prepared — no surprises
The CSAM owns the relationship; you own the outcome. Every client-facing moment must be coordinated. If the CSAM is surprised, the relationship is at risk. Pre-brief before every client interaction above routine status.
2
Signal-over-status updates
Updates to the CSAM must communicate what changes decisions — not just what happened. "The client re-asked the same question twice this week" is signal. "The call went well" is not.
3
Disagree in private, align in public
Disagreements between EDE and CSAM are resolved before the client interaction — never in front of the client. A unified front is non-negotiable. Raise concerns in the weekly check-in, not in the client call.
4
Outcome Owner call — shared between EDE and CSAM
The Outcome Owner call is a joint accountability moment. Both EDE and CSAM are present. The client sees a unified team with a single named owner. This is the primary trust-building cadence in the partnership model.
5
Weekly CSAM check-in — non-negotiable
The weekly check-in is not a status call — it is a signal-sharing session. What friction indicators did you observe? What communications are going out this week? What does the CSAM need to know before their next client interaction?
