📖 How to Use — EEP Skills Self-Assessment

A guide to scoring yourself accurately, reading the radar chart, and using your results for development planning.

🎯
Getting Started
How to use the assessment correctly
QWhat is this assessment measuring?
Your behavioral proficiency across the six EEP Skills Rubric dimensions. It is not a knowledge test — it measures what you do in real engagements, not what you know about the standard.
Key rule: A score without a specific behavioral example is not a score — it is a feeling. Score based on observable evidence from your recent engagements.
QHow often should I complete this assessment?
Every 30 days. Monthly scoring gives you enough time to observe real behavioral change between cycles. Your scores are saved automatically and persist between sessions.
QShould I score myself higher to look better?
No. The assessment only works if scored honestly. If the average score is rising but your customer signal is not improving, the scorecard is lying. The scorecard serves the standard, not the other way around. Score yourself where you actually are — not where you want to be.
📋
The Six Dimensions
What each dimension measures
QWhat does each dimension cover?
  • Signal Intelligence — Do you monitor the three signal streams weekly? Do you use signal language, not status language?
  • Ownership Clarity — Is there always one named Outcome Owner per engagement, communicated in writing before delivery begins?
  • Communication Discipline — Do you apply the Three Tests to every client-facing communication before sending?
  • Outcome Validation — Does the client articulate value in their own words before you declare the engagement closed?
  • Trust Architecture — Do you actively apply the CE Framework (Credibility → Reliability → Referenceability) to your engagements?
  • Program Stewardship — (L2+ only) Do you model, coach, sustain, and expand the EEP standard beyond your own engagements?
QWhat do the score levels mean?
ScoreLevelWhat it means

Reading the Radar Chart
How to interpret your competency profile
QHow do I read the radar chart?
Each axis represents one dimension. The further from the center, the higher the score (1 = center, 5 = outer edge). A balanced hexagon indicates consistent proficiency across all dimensions. Spikes indicate strengths; indentations indicate development areas.
QWhat should I focus on improving first?
Focus on your lowest-scoring dimension first — it is the weakest link in your delivery chain. A score of 1 or 2 in any dimension means that dimension is failing in practice. Pick one specific behavior to change this month and re-score in 30 days.
Tip: Don't try to improve all dimensions simultaneously. One focused behavior change per cycle compounds faster than diffuse effort across all six.
💾
Saving & Exporting
How your data is stored and shared
QWhere are my scores saved?
Scores are saved automatically to your browser's localStorage. They persist between sessions on the same browser and device. They are not sent to any server. Clearing your browser data will erase your scores.
QHow do I share my results with my manager?
Use the Export / Copy Summary button to copy your assessment results to the clipboard. Paste into an email, Teams message, or your bi-weekly check-in pre-read. Include your specific behavioral evidence for each score — not just the numbers.
