Signal Intelligence Tracker

Three signal streams — monitor weekly, surface within 48 hours, use signal language not status language

Signal vs. Status: "I'm seeing a pattern of the client re-asking questions we've already answered" (signal) vs. "Things are going well" (status). Signal language is observable, specific, and actionable.
📊 Stream 1: Customer Friction
Indicators that the client is experiencing confusion, dissatisfaction, or unmet expectation
Leading IndicatorsAmber Flag ConditionRed Flag Condition
Client re-asks questions already answered; client escalates without warning; client reduces engagement in status callsOne indicator in two consecutive weeksTwo+ indicators simultaneously; client mentions concern to non-engagement contact
💬 Stream 2: Communication Clarity
Indicators that our outbound messaging is landing with the intended meaning and producing the intended next action
Leading IndicatorsAmber Flag ConditionRed Flag Condition
Client requests clarification within 24hrs; client action delayed or incorrect; client forwards comms without adding contextTwo clarification requests in one week; one delayed action traceable to communication ambiguityClient expresses confusion about program status or next steps; missed action traceable to our communication failure
🎯 Stream 3: Ownership Follow-Through
Indicators that commitments made to the client are being tracked and closed on schedule
Leading IndicatorsAmber Flag ConditionRed Flag Condition
Committed action not completed on stated date; client asks "what happened with X?"; open issue count grows week-over-weekOne missed commitment with no proactive client notification; open issue count increasing for two consecutive weeksClient raises a missed commitment in a formal communication; open issues aged beyond agreed resolution window
📋 Three Communication Tests — Quick Reference
TEST 1
Repeat-Back Test
Could the client accurately restate the key content in their own words after one reading?
TEST 2
Stakes Test
Does the client understand what happens next if they do or do not take the requested action?
TEST 3
Next-Step Test
Is the immediate next action crystal clear — who does it, when, and how will completion be confirmed?

Three Tests Communication Checker

Apply all three tests to every client-facing communication before it is sent — no exceptions

Non-Negotiable II: Every client-facing communication passes the Three Communication Tests before it is sent. A communication that fails any one of the three tests is revised before delivery. No exceptions for seniority or schedule pressure.
✉️ Communication to Review
🔄 Test 1: Repeat-Back Test
Could the client accurately restate the key content in their own words after one reading?
Pass:
Core message in plain client language. No jargon, no unexplained acronyms, no passive constructions that obscure meaning.
Fail:
Message contains technical language the client does not use. Meaning requires interpretation.
⚡ Test 2: Stakes Test
Does the client understand what happens next if they do or do not take the requested action?
Pass:
Message explicitly states what the client needs to do, decide, or know — and what happens if they don't.
Fail:
Message is technically accurate but describes what happened without connecting it to the client's world or required action.
➡️ Test 3: Next-Step Test
Is the immediate next action crystal clear — who does it, when, and how will completion be confirmed?
Pass:
Message ends with a specific next step: who does what, by when, and what the client will hear next.
Fail:
Message ends with "please let us know if you have questions" or no defined next step.

CEES Scenario Practice

Interactive scenario practice — select the EEP-standard response, then reveal the scoring rationale

How to Use: Read each scenario carefully. Select the response that best reflects the EEP standard. Click "Reveal Answer" to see the rubric-based scoring and explanation. Use these in bi-weekly CEES Sessions.

Scenario 1: The Missed Commitment

Signal Intelligence · Ownership Clarity
During your weekly Signal Review, you notice that a deliverable you committed to the client for Tuesday has not been completed — it's now Thursday. The client has not yet raised it. What do you do?
✓ Correct: Option C
The EEP standard requires proactive communication before the client raises the issue. A missed commitment that the team surfaces proactively — with a new committed date — is a trust-building event. A missed commitment that the client surfaces first is a trust-damaging event. The difference is not the missed commitment. The difference is who surfaces it first. Option A is a signal failure. Option B fails the Next-Step Test. Option D delays client communication unnecessarily.

Scenario 2: The Status Update Email

Communication Discipline
You've drafted a weekly status update email. The Repeat-Back Test passes. The Next-Step Test fails — there's no clear action requested from the client. What do you do?
✓ Correct: Option B
The Three Tests apply to every client-facing communication — major or minor. There are no exceptions for "just a status update." Option C fails the Next-Step Test — "please let us know if you have any questions" is not a specific next action; it's a passive invitation that produces no observable client behavior. Option B correctly applies the test: revise until the communication passes, then send.

Scenario 3: The Signal Review

Signal Intelligence
During your Weekly Signal Review, a team member says: "The client seems happy — no complaints this week, the calls have been going well, and they seemed positive in the last meeting." How do you respond as the facilitator?
✓ Correct: Option B
"The client seems happy" is status language — it is a subjective impression, not an observable signal. The Signal Review is a diagnostic session, not a sentiment check. Option B is the correct facilitation move: name the language pattern, redirect to the three-stream model, and ask for observable behavioral evidence. "What did the client do or say?" is the right question. The answer to that question is a signal. The impression is not.

Scenario 4: The Engagement Close

Outcome Validation
Your engagement is wrapping up. All deliverables have been completed and accepted. The client has signed the milestone completion document. Your manager is asking you to close the engagement in the system. The client has not yet been asked the three validation questions. What do you do?
✓ Correct: Option C
Non-Negotiable Commitment III is explicit: no engagement is administratively closed without a validated client articulation of value received. A signed milestone document is not the same as a client who can articulate what they received and why it mattered. Option A confuses task completion with value validation. Option B defers the validation indefinitely. Option D attempts to route around the standard through seniority — the charter is explicit that seniority does not constitute an exemption.

Engagement Tracker
Per-dispatch delivery step checklist
QWhat are the 8 delivery steps?
  • SCOPE — Scoping call completed, Recap email sent
  • PREEMAIL — Pre-engagement/Pre-Poll email sent
  • UPLOAD — Workshop materials uploaded early
  • TECHCONTACT — Correct technical contact entered
  • POLL — Adaptive polling executed
  • POSTEMAIL — Daily/Final Recap email sent
  • CLOSEOUT — Closeout motion completed
  • SURVEY — Survey/closeout email sent
QWhat is the difference between Validated, Claimed, and Missing?
Validated ✓ — A system signal exists (sent email, calendar event, upload). Claimed ~ — Self-reported only, no system evidence. Missing ✗ — Required step, past due, no signal. Only Validated steps count toward your completion score.
Tip: Claimed steps show as "unverified" — always aim for Validated status.
QHow is the completion percentage calculated?
Completion = Validated steps ÷ Total required steps. Claimed steps do not count. The progress bar updates in real time as you mark steps.
📡
Signal Intelligence
Three signal streams with flag detection
QWhat are the three signal streams?
  • Customer Friction — Indicators of confusion, dissatisfaction, or unmet expectation
  • Communication Clarity — Whether outbound messaging is landing correctly
  • Ownership Follow-Through — Whether commitments are being tracked and closed
QWhen should I set Amber vs Red?
Amber 🟡 — One indicator present; investigate and address before it escalates. Red 🔴 — Two or more indicators simultaneously, or client has raised concern to a non-engagement contact. Red = same-day escalation required.
Tip: When in doubt, set Amber. The cost of investigating a false Amber is one conversation. The cost of ignoring a real Amber is a trust failure.
QWhat is signal language vs status language?
Status: "Things are going well." — Zero information density.
Signal: "The client's VP stopped attending our weekly calls — that's a friction signal." — Changes a decision. Always use signal language in your observations.

Compliance Scorecard
0–100% weighted compliance scoring
QHow is the compliance score calculated?
Eight weighted items sum to 100%: Scoping Recap (15%), Pre-Poll (20%), Data Sheet (10%), RMOT in subject (10%), PCC in CC (10%), Delivery Recap (15%), Follow-Up (10%), Time-keeping tags (10%). Check each item as you complete it.
QWhat is the passing threshold?
The target threshold is 70% — shown on the progress bar. Below 70% opens a compliance gap. The goal is 100% on every engagement.
QDo I need to re-score for each engagement?
Yes — the scorecard reflects your current engagement. Reset it at the start of each new dispatch by unchecking all items. Your score is stored in your browser session.

Three Tests Checker
Validate communications before sending
QWhat are the Three Tests?
  • Repeat-Back Test — Could the client accurately restate the key content in their own words?
  • Stakes Test — Does the client understand what happens next if they do or don't act?
  • Next-Step Test — Is the immediate next action crystal clear — who, when, how confirmed?
QAre there exceptions to the Three Tests?
No. There are no exceptions for seniority or schedule pressure. A communication that fails any one test is revised before delivery. The moments when you're most tempted to skip the tests are the moments when they matter most.
QDo the Three Tests apply to internal communications?
Yes — the Three Tests apply to internal handoffs as well as client-facing communications. Internal clarity is the leading indicator of external clarity.
