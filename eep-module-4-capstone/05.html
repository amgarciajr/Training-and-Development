Catching Drift Before It Becomes Culture

Behavioral drift is incremental, invisible from inside, and always accumulates

Drift Unchecked
How it accumulates silently
Drift is incremental — it never announces itself; it accumulates one skipped step at a time.
Team stops running CEES Sessions after major delivery events — first because the calendar is tight, then because it feels normal.
Reconstruction shrinks to a single question; lessons become generic and disconnected from real delivery.
Invisible from inside — those living the drift cannot see it; only those holding the standard can.
Pattern: "We've been really busy — the sessions are still happening, they're just more efficient now."
Trainer Response
Reference awareness and precise naming
Maintain reference awareness — hold the standard in mind even when the team has stopped measuring against it.
Name drift specifically and early — not accusatorially, but precisely. Vague concern is not enough.
Ask directly: "What is getting in the way of the preparation?" Surface the real obstacle before another month passes.
Early correction is a conversation. Late correction is a conflict. The timing of the intervention determines its cost.
Principle: "I want to name something I'm noticing — without blame. That's a pattern. Let's be honest about it now."
EEP Trainer Standard: Early correction is a conversation — late correction is a conflict. Name it early. Name it precisely.
