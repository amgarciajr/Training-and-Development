What Is CEEDS?

Module 2 · EEP Framework

CEEDS — the Customer Engagement Excellence Delivery Standard — is the team-level operating framework of the EEP. Where EDEv4 governs what happens at the engagement level, CEEDS governs how the team operates day-to-day as a cultural and organizational unit, establishing the norms, commitments, and learning rhythms that allow EDEv4 to function reliably across a portfolio.

01 · Scope

Team-Level Operating Framework

CEEDS governs daily team behavior — not individual engagement mechanics. It defines how a team operates as a cultural and organizational unit, setting shared norms and commitments that persist across every client engagement.

02 · Function

Translates EDEv4 Into Recurring Practice

CEEDS is the learning and reinforcement architecture of the EEP. It converts EDEv4 principles into teachable, scenario-based learning experiences that teams can practice, repeat, and internalize over time.

03 · Structure

Four Pillars + Five Operating Motions

The framework is organized around four foundational pillars — explicit trade-offs the team commits to — and five operating motions that define how those commitments are enacted in day-to-day delivery work.

04 · Mechanism

CEES Sessions: The Primary Learning Vehicle

CEES Sessions are the structured, recurring team meetings where CEEDS comes to life. They serve as the primary mechanism for calibration, scenario practice, and reinforcement of engagement excellence standards.

Key Insight: CEEDS makes EDEv4 function reliably across a portfolio — not heroically on isolated engagements. It is the difference between individual excellence and systemic team performance.
