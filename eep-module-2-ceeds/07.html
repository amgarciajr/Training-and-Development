EEP Training Delivery Guide · Section 4

Resistance Patterns & Facilitation Scripts

Pattern 01
Standard Acceptance with Caveat
"I get the standard, but our account is different." Participant accepts the framework in principle while carving out their own exception.
"What specifically makes this account different from the standard? Let's test that assumption against the model."
Pattern 02
Status Drift in Signal Review
Senior participant reframes signal data through their own account experience, shifting the review from signals to status narrative.
"That's status language. What specific client behavior did you observe this week — what did they do or say?"
Pattern 03
Outcome Owner as Label
Role authority is used to override the standard — "I'm the outcome owner, so I decide how this works on my account."
"Ownership defines accountability, not the standard itself. The standard applies to how we deliver, not who decides."
Pattern 04
Blame in CEES Sessions
Attribution to a specific person's failure derails the session — the team focuses on who caused the issue rather than what the signal reveals.
"We're not here to assign fault. What does this signal tell us about the system, and what do we adjust?"
Pattern 05
Quiet Drift
No stated resistance, but the program atrophies in substance — sessions become shorter, shallower, and eventually stop producing real outputs.
"Let's check against the WGLL standard. What was the last concrete adjustment this team made based on a CEES session?"
Pattern 06
Not My Scope
Scope exclusion removes team members from the standard — "That function isn't part of my workstream, so the standard doesn't apply to me."
"If they interact with the client or influence delivery outcomes, they're in scope. Scope is defined by client impact."
