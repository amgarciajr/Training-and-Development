CEEDS in Practice — Scenario Exercise

CEES Scenario 4
The Signal Review
Training Delivery Guide · Section 5
Scenario Prompt
During a Weekly Signal Review, a team member says: "The client seems happy — no complaints this week, the calls have been going well, and they seemed positive in the last meeting."   How do you respond as the Engagement Lead facilitating the review?
A
Accept & Move On
Accept the update — positive sentiment is treated as a valid signal
No follow-up questions — move on to the next agenda item
Status language accepted — "seems happy" logged as engagement health indicator
Missed learning opportunity — team never develops signal observation skills
VS
B
Redirect to Signals — CORRECT
Name the pattern: "That's status language — not a signal."
Redirect to three-stream model: friction, clarity, and follow-through
Ask the signal question: "What did the client do or say that you're reading as positive?"
Observable behavior only: specific client actions = signals; general impressions = status
Facilitator Script
"What specific signals did you observe this week across the three streams — friction, clarity, and follow-through?"
Status Language "The client seems happy" — impression, not evidence
Signal Language "What did the client do or say?" — specific, observable, actionable
Key Rule If you can't point to a behavior, it's not a signal — it's a feeling
