Trust in Practice — Two Scenarios

Scenario A
The Escalation That Built Trust
1
Critical issue detected mid-project — integration failure identified early by the engagement team.
2
Client notified immediately — no delay, no minimizing. Full transparency on scope and stakes.
3
Outcome Owner named — clear accountability assigned with a defined resolution path communicated.
4
Issue closed on committed timeline — follow-through demonstrated under real pressure.
5
Engagement experienced a significant problem — yet the client witnessed exactly the behavior needed.
Trust Outcome
↑ Trust Increased
Transparency + accountability + follow-through
VS
Scenario B
The Smooth Delivery That Didn't
1
Delivered on time, on budget, to spec — technically flawless execution by every measurable standard.
2
Client never oriented to purpose — what was being built and why was never clearly communicated.
3
Milestone comms technically accurate but incomprehensible — no meaningful context provided throughout.
4
At closure, client cannot articulate what they received or what value was delivered.
5
Engagement was technically successful — the trust relationship was never built.
Trust Outcome
✗ Trust Not Built
No reference given · No renewal conversation starts

CE Framework Principle: The goal is not a problem-free engagement — it is an engagement where the team's behavior under pressure builds the trust that creates references and renewals.
